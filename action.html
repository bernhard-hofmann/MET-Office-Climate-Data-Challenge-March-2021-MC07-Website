IPCC net zero by 2050

In order for us to achieve the IPCC Paris Agreement target of a 1.5°C warming target by year 2100 we need to achieve net zero emissions by 2050 which will require each of us to take action.

The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) is the United Nations body for assessing the science related to climate change.

The clock is ticking

Take Action

It's easy to feel powerless given the situation we face but, for better or for worse, we are already individually contributing to the climate changes we see. This page will help you make decisions for the better to improve our world for ourselves and future generations.

Visit

Visit the outdoors to appreciate the health-giving benefits.

Educate

Educate yourself and those around you, when possible.

BE MINDFUL OF BEING AS ‘GREEN’ AS IN YOUR POWER TO BE

Reducing emissions is the number one way we can limit the impacts of climate change on the oceans. It is everyone’s responsibility to do their bit.

From how you:

  • Run your home
  • Personal travel
  • Where you buy your food, clothes and other goods
  • What you eat
  • Invest your money...

Industries must change their practises, and governments must fund and support green growth and economies, renewable policies and legislation.

Vote

If it's possible for you to do so, go out and vote for those political parties that support action on climate change, lobby your MP or local politician.
